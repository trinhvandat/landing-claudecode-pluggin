# Persona Framework

Reference for Step 2-3 of the audience-persona skill. Use this to build realistic, actionable buyer personas.

## What Makes a Good Persona

A good persona is **specific enough to write ad copy for**. If you can't write a headline that speaks directly to this persona, the persona is too vague.

### Test: Can you complete this sentence?
> "{Persona name} would click on this ad because ___"

If you can't fill the blank with a specific reason, your persona needs more detail.

## Building Personas from Keyword Data

Keywords are behavioral signals. They reveal what people want, fear, and prioritize.

### Keyword → Persona Mapping

| Keyword Signal | What It Reveals | Persona Trait |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Price keywords ("giá", "bao nhiêu", "cost") | Budget-conscious | Income-sensitive, needs value justification |
| Quality keywords ("tốt nhất", "uy tín", "best") | Risk-averse, quality-first | Higher income, fears bad outcome more than cost |
| Urgency keywords ("gấp", "ngay", "emergency") | Time-pressured | Will pay premium for speed, low patience for friction |
| Comparison keywords ("so sánh", "vs", "review") | Research-driven | Methodical, needs data, longer decision cycle |
| Location keywords ("gần đây", "near me", "ở") | Convenience-focused | Values proximity, may prefer local/trusted provider |
| How-to keywords ("cách", "hướng dẫn", "how") | Early-stage, educational | Not ready to buy yet — informational intent |
| Brand keywords (competitor names) | Aware of alternatives | Switching consideration, needs differentiation |

## Pain Points: How to Make Them Specific

### BAD Pain Points (too generic)
- "Wants a good service"
- "Doesn't want to waste money"
- "Looking for quality"

### GOOD Pain Points (specific and emotional)
- "Terrified that the implant will fail after spending $3,000"
- "Exhausted from comparing 10+ clinics on Google, can't tell which is legit"
- "Embarrassed about missing teeth but afraid surgery will be visible to coworkers"

### Formula for Specific Pain Points
```
[EMOTION] + [SPECIFIC SCENARIO] + [CONSEQUENCE THEY FEAR]

"Worried that [specific thing] will lead to [specific bad outcome]"
"Frustrated because [specific situation] and can't find [specific solution]"
"Afraid that [specific action] will result in [specific loss]"
```

## Objections: The 5 Universal Categories

Every purchase objection falls into one of these:

1. **PRICE**: "Is it worth the money?" → Address with: value comparison, ROI, payment plans
2. **TRUST**: "Can I trust this provider?" → Address with: social proof, credentials, guarantees
3. **TIME**: "Is now the right time?" → Address with: urgency, cost-of-waiting, limited offers
4. **NEED**: "Do I really need this?" → Address with: pain amplification, future consequences
5. **FIT**: "Is this the right solution for ME?" → Address with: personalization, case studies, segmentation

For each persona, identify their PRIMARY objection category. This determines:
- Which trust signals go near the CTA
- What the FAQ section addresses
- How the ad description is framed

## Buying Triggers: What Makes Them Act NOW

| Trigger Type | Examples | Use In |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Pain escalation | Symptoms worsen, problem becomes urgent | Ad urgency copy |
| Social pressure | Event coming up, others have done it | Ad description |
| Financial window | Bonus received, tax refund, year-end budget | Offer timing |
| External deadline | Insurance expiring, season changing | Countdown/urgency on LP |
| Comparison fatigue | Tired of researching, ready to decide | CTA simplicity |
| Recommendation | Friend/family suggested it | Testimonial emphasis |
